Who we are

About Us

Volunteers play a crucial role in disaster management, providing assistance and support to affected communities during and after disasters.

Rescues during disasters require a coordinated effort involving various emergency responders, volunteers, and organizations.

Our Mission

The mission of volunteers in disaster management is multifaceted and encompasses various roles and responsibilities aimed at providing assistance, support, and relief to affected communities before, during, and after disasters.

Overall, the mission of volunteers in disaster management is to save lives, alleviate suffering, and support the recovery and rebuilding of communities affected by disasters through their dedication, compassion, and commitment to serving others in times of need.

Our Values

the value of volunteers in disaster management lies in their selfless service, diverse skills and expertise, local knowledge and connections, and unwavering commitment to supporting affected communities in their time of need. Their contributions are essential for saving lives, alleviating suffering, and facilitating the recovery and rebuilding process in the aftermath of disasters.

Volunteers in disaster management bring invaluable contributions that enrich response efforts and support affected communities in numerous ways.

Our History

The history of volunteer involvement in disaster management is rich and diverse, dating back centuries and evolving alongside changes in societal structures, governance, and humanitarian practices.

Throughout history, volunteers have played a crucial role in disaster management, providing vital support to affected communities and contributing to the resilience and recovery of societies in the face of adversity.

Safety and Coordination

Sharing live location data can help ensure the safety of volunteers by providing real-time information about their whereabouts in hazardous or chaotic environments. This information allows coordination teams to track volunteer movements, assess risks, and deploy resources more effectively.

Empower communities

Empowering communities during disasters involves equipping them with the knowledge, resources, and support needed to prepare for, respond to, and recover from emergencies.

Mapping and Geospatial Analysis

Live location information can be used to create real-time maps and geospatial analyses of the disaster area, providing valuable insights into population movements, infrastructure damage, access routes, and areas of concentration for relief activities.Volunteers sharing live location data contribute to a better understanding of the evolving situation on the ground. It helps relief agencies.

Food distribution

Volunteers help distribute food to individuals and families facing hunger through food banks, soup kitchens, community pantries, and mobile food distribution programs. They assist in sorting, packaging, and distributing food items to ensure equitable access for those in need.

Shelter and Evacuation Support

Volunteers help set up and manage shelters for displaced individuals and families. They provide comfort, organize supplies, offer information and assistance, and ensure the safety and well-being of evacuees.

Education and Advocacy

Volunteers raise awareness about food insecurity issues, advocate for policy changes to address root causes of hunger, and provide education on nutrition, budgeting, and food management skills to empower individuals and families to make healthier food choices and stretch their food budgets.
